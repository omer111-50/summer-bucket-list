# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A tight group of Muslim lads, 21–25, based in Oldham, Greater Manchester. They default to the same restaurants every weekend — not from lack of interest in doing more, but from lack of a frictionless way to decide. They use this on their phones, usually in a group chat moment: "what are we doing this weekend?" Context is casual, social, mobile-first.

## Product Purpose

A curated summer activity guide for Manchester and the North West — 64 handpicked outdoor and adventure activities across the Peak District, Lake District, Yorkshire Dales, and North Wales. The job: make it easy to pick something, commit, and go. Success looks like less defaulting to restaurants and more actual weekends worth talking about.

## Brand Personality

Bold, playful, alive. High energy without being aggressive. Summer-feeling. Something you'd genuinely share with the group. Opinionated curation, not a directory.

## Anti-references

- **Generic travel apps** — TripAdvisor, Google Maps grid. Impersonal, commercial, review-driven. This is handpicked, not crowd-sourced.
- **Wellness/mindfulness** — Calm, Headspace, soft pastels, slow tempo, self-improvement tone. This is about doing stuff, not reflecting on it.
- **Corporate SaaS dashboards** — over-engineered, metric-heavy, serious. Not a tool for work.
- **Hype streetwear/gaming** — loud neon, aggressive energy, trying too hard. Bold, but not performative.

## Design Principles

1. **Made for one group, feels personal.** Every word and design choice should feel like it was written by someone who actually knows the lads — not a product team, not an algorithm.
2. **Opinionated, not comprehensive.** 64 picks, not 640. The curation is the product. Design should reinforce that confidence.
3. **Decision support, not decision overload.** Filters exist to narrow fast, not to expose complexity. The UI should make choosing feel easy and quick.
4. **Summer energy in the details.** The dark-forest palette and amber are the base. Personality lives in motion, copy, and the moments of delight — not in adding more colour.
5. **Phone-first, always.** Everything is designed for one hand, a quick scroll, and a group-chat share. Desktop is an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2 AA minimum. Halal filter is a first-class feature, not an afterthought. Reduced-motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Verified with pa11y and axe (see README for commands).
